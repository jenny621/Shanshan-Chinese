AR with Continuing Mandarin Chinese

Why this project?

Continuing Mandarin Chinese is a textbook for intermediate learners of Mandarin Chinese. It provides nice videos of the textbook dialogues on the publisher's website and YouTube. I wanted to develop an augmented reality (AR) feature that links textbook lesson images to corresponding dialogue videos, enabling learners to scan images and instantly view the associated video content. Hopefully, this makes access to the accompanying videos easier, encouraging learners to get more exposure to the sound of the language, as well as the sight of the interlocutors and the surrounding environment.

User's Story

Name: Emma

Age: 19

Occupation: student (business major) and waitress

Hometown: Cleveland, Ohio

Emma is a sophomore and is from within state. She lives a busy life, taking 15 credit hours of class this semester--including 5 credits of Intermediate Chinese--while working 20 hours a week. She is committed to learning Chinese because she hopes one day she can use the language to do business in China. In her spare time, Emma enjoys cooking food with her friends.

Emma's goals with Chinese are: (1) to get more exposure to authentic Chinese, and (2) to learn Chinese in an effective and time-efficient way.

Specifically, as a busy university student joggling study and work, she wants to have instant access to the textbook's accompanying digital materials whenever she gets spare time here and there to study Chinese with the textbook. She does not want to waste time finding the publisher's website and locating the video she needs for a specific lesson.

10:00 pm Study Chinese Emma is tired but she needs to prepare for tomorrow's Chinese class. She takes out her textbook, turns to Lesson 1 Part 2, and scans the image with her iPhone. In no time, her phone is playing the corresponding dialogue. Since this is a new dialogue, Emma occasionally encounters unfamiliar words or phrases that she does not comprehend the first time she heard them. To hear a new word or phrase again, she simply hits the "Backward 5s" button. Sometimes, she hits the "Play/Pause" button to pause the video so she can turn to a different page to read explanations. As she becomes more familiar with the dialogue, she uses the "Forward 5s" button to skip the opening music and between-line context footage. Thanks to this AR feature, Emma saves time from navigating the publisher's website and the video, which helps her complete her preparation in a shorter amount of time.
