Who is Shanshan Chinese for?

The primary goals of this website are to help learners of Mandarin Chinese explore fun and effective ways to learn Chinese and to help them find Chinese language teachers and other learners to interact with and stay motivated together. So if you are a learner of Chinese—whatever proficiency level you are currently at, and whether you are taking structured Chinese classes at a university or simply learning Chinese on your own—you will find something useful on this website. I hope you enjoy the lifelong journey of learning to communicate in Chinese!

Do we still need to learn foreign languages when AI tranlation systems have been improving quickly?

The past couple of years have seen several large AI-powered translation systems appear in the market. So a natural question to ask is: What role, if any, does foreign-language education play in a world where translation technologies have been advancing at breakneck speed? This is an important question for foreign-language educators to ponder, because it directly affects what content they teach in class, as well as how they teach it. To put it bluntly, if we accept that AI technologies will outpace the learning of words and grammar rules by a Chinese major or an enthusiastic autonomous learner of Chinese, should they simply take advantage of technological advancement and speak comfortably in their mother tongue without ever bothering to learn a foreign language?

I think the answer depends on what levels of communication one wants to achieve. If one's goal is to exchange information with people speaking a foreign language on a sporadic basis, then a machine translator should suffice. On the other hand, if one's goal is to share experiences and build long-term relationships with people from different cultures—that is, people with different ways of living and making sense of the world—then I believe learning a foreign language is the path that can lead to that goal most effectively and most profoundly.

Based on this understanding of why we need to learn a foreign language like Mandarin Chinese, everything on this website focuses on cultivating your ability to communicate in Chinese culture or Taiwanese culture through contextualized learning. That is, no more memorization of isolated words or grammar patterns. Instead, contextual factors like time, place, who you're talking to, who else is present, and so on, are included so you know what you need to do—including what to say and how to say it—to achieve your communication goal(s) in a given situation.
